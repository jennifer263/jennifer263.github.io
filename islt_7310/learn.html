Unit 1-First Presidency

Main Ideas

  1. President Washington and members of Congress established a new national government.
  2. Treasury Secretary Alexander Hamilton devoloped a financial plan for the national government.
  3. The United States faced significant foreign and domestic challenges under Washington.
  4. The development of political parties in the United SAtates contributed to differing ideas about the role of the federal government.

Skill Mastery: Explain connections between historical context and peoples' perspective at the time in American History. Strict v. Loose Interpretation of the Constitution.

Learning Targets:

  1. Understand and explain contest and perspective.
  2. Understand opposing perspectives on the same historical event.
  3. Make connections between historical events and peoples' perscpectives.

Unit 2-Jefferson Era

Main Ideas

  1. Thomas Jefferson's election began a new Era in American government.
  2. Under President Jefferson's leadership, the United States added the Louisiana Territory.
  3. Challenges at home and abroad led the United States to decalre war on Great Britain.
  4. Great Britain and the United States went to battle in the War of 1812.

Skill Mastery: Analyze laws, policies, and processes to determine how governmental systems affect individuals and groups in society in American history prior to c. 1870.

Learning Targets:

  1. Summarize and form an opinion as to what the law, policy or process means.
  2. Define the principles of government (federalism, popular sovereignty, limited government, checks and balances, judicial review and separation of powers).
  3. Students will cite evidence to support principles of government.

Unit 3-Jackson Era

Main Ideas

  1. The United States peacefully settled disputes with foreign powers.
  2. A rising sense of national unity allowed some regional differences to be set aside and national interests to be served.
  3. As the United States grew developments in many cultural areas contributed to the creation of a new American Identity.

Skill Mastery: Using an inquiry lens, develop compelling questions about American history prior to 1870, to determine helpful resources and consider multiple points of view respesented in resources.

Learning Targets:

  1. Create a compelling question.
  2. Identify credible sources with multiple points of view.
  3. Identify how points of view change over time.
